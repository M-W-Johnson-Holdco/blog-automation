## What Hail Damage Signs Should Homeowners in Pickens and Dawson Counties Look For?

Look first for granule loss, circular bruises on shingles, and dents in soft metal like gutters, vents, and flashing. Hail rarely punches a clean hole — instead it bruises the mat beneath the granules, and those bruises fail months later during ordinary rain. The February 26 storm produced stones "ranging in size from peas to ping pong balls," according to Pickens County emergency officials, with enough accumulation to close Highway 136 east of Jasper (Source: [cbs46.com](https://www.cbs46.com/2026/02/27/many-north-georgia-reportedly-seeing-hail-severe-thunderstorm-moves-through), February 2026). From the ground, scan your gutters and downspouts for dents and pooled granules — the earliest visible clue. On the roof itself, damage clusters on the slope that faced the storm. Document everything with dated photos before you touch a shingle.

## How Do Aging Shingles and Failed Flashing Turn Hail Into a Denied Claim?

Insurers frequently deny hail claims when they can attribute leaks to age or deferred maintenance rather than the storm — which is why roof condition determines whether your claim succeeds. Hail damage roof repair depends on proving the storm caused the damage, and worn shingles, brittle flashing, or clogged valleys give adjusters a reason to point elsewhere.

Bruised shingles that already lost granules to age look ambiguous under inspection. Cracked or lifted flashing around chimneys and vents leaks whether or not hail hit it. Debris-packed valleys and gutters that dented in the storm can mask the exact impact points an adjuster needs to see. A professional roof inspection with close-range documentation separates fresh hail bruising from ordinary wear — the distinction that keeps a claim alive.

### What does hail damage actually do to asphalt shingles?
Hail bruises the fiberglass mat beneath the granules, crushing the protective layer and exposing the shingle to UV and water. Stones the size of ping-pong balls — reported across Pickens and Dawson counties in February 2026 — routinely cause this bruising even when the roof looks intact from the ground. The bruise fails weeks or months later, letting water into the deck. That delay is why a professional inspection right after a storm matters more than a driveway glance.

### Should I get my roof inspected if I only see dented gutters?
Yes — dented gutters are one of the clearest signals that hail large enough to bruise shingles hit your home. Aluminum gutters and downspouts dent at smaller hail sizes than shingles show visible damage, so they act as an early warning. If your gutters took hits during the February 2026 storm that dented cars across north Georgia, your roof slopes almost certainly did too. Schedule a full roof inspection rather than assuming the roof escaped.

### What's the difference between cosmetic and functional hail damage?
Functional damage compromises the roof's ability to shed water — bruised mats, fractured shingles, or damaged flashing — while cosmetic damage only affects appearance, like minor denting on impact-resistant shingles. Insurers pay for functional damage but may exclude cosmetic-only marks depending on your policy. The problem is that bruising which looks cosmetic often becomes functional as it degrades. A close-range professional inspection, not a ground-level guess, is the only reliable way to tell them apart.

Often yes — many policies pay actual cash value first, then release the withheld recoverable depreciation once you complete the repair and submit final invoices. This means finishing the work and documenting it can recover the difference between depreciated value and full replacement cost. Keep every invoice and completion record.
