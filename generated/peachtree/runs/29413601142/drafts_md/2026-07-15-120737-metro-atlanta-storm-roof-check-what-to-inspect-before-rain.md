## How do aging shingles turn a routine storm into water damage?

Aging shingles turn routine rain into water damage because their protective granules wear away and their sealant bond weakens, leaving the mat exposed and the shingle prone to wind lift. Once a shingle loses its seal, wind-driven rain — the kind CBS News Atlanta is forecasting this weekend — pushes moisture beneath the surface and into the underlayment and decking (Source: [cbsnews.com](https://www.cbsnews.com/atlanta/video/stormy-weekend-ahead-atlanta-forecast-rainfall-thunderstorm-timing/), July 2026). Deferred maintenance compounds the problem: cracked flashing, clogged gutters, and worn pipe boots each create an entry point that a healthy roof would resist. On a 20-plus-year roof in East Cobb or Chamblee, these small failures often occur together, which is why a professional roof inspection matters more than a quick glance from the driveway. ### Can wind-driven rain damage my roof even if no shingles blow off?

Yes — wind-driven rain can break a shingle's sealant bond without removing the shingle, creating a hidden leak path. The failure often stays invisible until the next rain pushes water beneath the surface. That's why a post-storm inspection matters even when your roof looks intact from the street.
