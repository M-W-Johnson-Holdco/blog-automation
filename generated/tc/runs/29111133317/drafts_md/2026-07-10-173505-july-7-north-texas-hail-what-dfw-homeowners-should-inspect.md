## What kind of roof damage does a hail and wind event like July 7 cause in Dallas-Fort Worth?

Hail and wind damage roofs in two distinct ways — hail bruises and fractures shingles while wind lifts and creases them, breaking the seal that keeps water out. The July 7 First Alert Weather Day flagged both threats across North Texas (Source: [cbsnews.com](https://www.cbsnews.com/texas/video/hail-wind-damage-across-for-some-parts-of-north-texas-possible/), July 2026). In Dallas-Fort Worth's mature suburbs, most homes carry three-tab or architectural asphalt shingles that lose granules and impact resistance as they age, so a moderate hailstorm can cause meaningful damage to a 15-year-old roof that a newer roof would shrug off. Wind damage is especially common along ridge lines and roof edges where uplift concentrates. The practical takeaway: check for scattered granules in gutters and downspouts, and look for shingle tabs that appear lifted, curled, or missing.
